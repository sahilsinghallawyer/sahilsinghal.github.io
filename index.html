Profile
Research Focus
My work centres on the evolution of corporate misconduct — from subtle pressure and incentives inside organisations to the eventual involvement of regulators, investigators, and courts.

I am particularly interested in:
  • How bribery and corruption emerge inside otherwise “controlled” systems.
  • How fraud patterns repeat across industries and geographies.
  • How regulators form views on intent, credibility, and cooperation.
  • How organisations behave when confronted with scrutiny or crisis.
Current Direction
Fraulex — Private Intelligence Lab
I currently build and run Fraulex, a quiet research-led platform focused on:
  • Pattern-based fraud & corruption intelligence.
  • Research on regulatory reasoning and enforcement trends.
  • Simulation of crisis and decision-making under pressure.

The intent is to provide a disciplined, low-noise view of risk: not as checklists, but as evolving patterns of behaviour and oversight.

Themes
Areas of Ongoing Study
A non-exhaustive set of questions I work on:
  • What early signals appear before a major corruption or fraud case?
  • How do internal structures enable, ignore, or misread those signals?
  • How do anti-bribery regimes (FCPA, UKBA, PC Act, OECD standards) shape organisational behaviour?
  • How do cross-border investigations develop when multiple agencies are involved?
  • What does “adequate procedures” mean in practice, beyond policy language?
